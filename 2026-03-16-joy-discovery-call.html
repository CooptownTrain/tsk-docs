Executive Summary

▾

Who Joy Is

Event planning platform (weddings, corporate events). Claims 55M users across 150+ countries. YC W16 alumni. Their hotel product auto-surfaces accommodations near event venues and earns commission on bookings. Claims ~$400K/day in booking volume.

What They Proposed

Explore an AI partnership where TSK could leverage Joy's event planning AI and data layer for a luxury guest concierge experience. Separately, list TSK properties on their accommodation platform as a distribution channel.

What We Could Want

Access to Joy's event data layer and AI capabilities to power a TSK-branded luxury concierge. Joy's real value may be in their proprietary data (6K+ events, 55M claimed users, booking history, supplier network) rather than the AI models themselves. Distribution is a secondary benefit.

Where It Stands

Richie committed to sending the distribution agreement by March 15-16. Not yet received. The AI partnership has not been discussed in detail yet and is the higher-priority conversation. Next step is reviewing the distribution agreement once received and scheduling a follow-up focused on the AI opportunity.

Key Observations

The AI partnership is the primary opportunity. Joy's potential value is in their proprietary event data layer (booking patterns, supplier relationships, event logistics) rather than the AI models themselves, which appear to be off-the-shelf (Intercom Fin, OpenAI). Understanding exactly what Joy owns vs. licenses is the key question for the next conversation. The distribution side is straightforward and could serve as an entry point to the broader relationship. Several data points shared on the call could not be independently verified (see unverified claims). The typical Joy customer budget ($35-45K) may also be below TSK's property floor for top-tier listings, though the high-end segment ($500K+) could align.

Potential Deal Structure

▾

Two potential layers discussed. The AI partnership is the higher-priority opportunity for TSK. Distribution could serve as the entry point to the relationship.

Layer 1: Distribution (Accommodation Listings)

  • TSK properties would appear when a Joy user's event is near a TSK location
  • Users book directly through Joy's platform
  • Integration path: Richie referenced "Zentrum Hub" as the connector. This could not be verified (see unverified claims). Needs clarification.
  • Revenue model: Richie asked whether we prefer commission-based or net rate. Both options are on the table.
  • Agreement status: Richie committed to sending the agreement by March 15-16 for our review. Not yet received.

Types of Terms to Consider

  • Revenue model (commission vs. net rate vs. rev-share)
  • Exclusivity and territorial scope
  • Minimum booking thresholds or performance commitments
  • Brand guidelines and listing presentation standards
  • Data sharing and reporting obligations
  • Term length and termination provisions

Layer 2: AI Partnership (Exploratory)

  • Concept: TSK could leverage Joy's AI and event data for a luxury guest concierge
  • Richie described an upcoming product where users prompt an event vision and AI builds the full plan
  • Research finding: Joy's support AI appears to be Intercom Fin (off-the-shelf). Product AI appears to use OpenAI. The potential differentiator is Joy's proprietary event data layer, not the AI models themselves.
  • No terms, pricing, or timeline were discussed. See AI partnership models for potential structure types.

Types of Terms to Consider

  • Partnership model (licensing, white-label, API access, rev-share, co-development)
  • IP ownership and data rights
  • Scope of AI capabilities provided
  • Pricing structure (fixed fee, per-use, revenue-based)
  • Exclusivity in the luxury travel vertical
  • Integration requirements and technical dependencies

Reference: Potential AI Partnership Models

▾

Industry context: AI in hospitality is a $20.5B market at 30% CAGR. IDC projects agentic AI will redefine travel in 2026. Canary Technologies just launched the first hospitality AI agent builder. Alternatives to Joy exist.

ModelHow It Could WorkConsiderations
Licensing TSK licenses Joy's AI + data layer. TSK owns the guest interface. Offers control. Requires clear data rights agreement.
White-Label Joy provides a branded AI assistant. TSK applies its own branding. Fastest path. Less control over the experience.
Pay-Per-Use API calls per guest interaction. No upfront commitment. Low risk for testing. May not scale efficiently.
Rev-Share AI drives bookings or upsells. Joy receives a percentage of AI-attributed value. Aligns incentives. Requires attribution tracking.
Co-Development Build a luxury-specific AI together. Shared or split IP. Highest potential value. Most complex. IP boundaries need careful definition.

Other players in this space: AI-Concierge, Canary Technologies AI Agent Studio, and Intercom Fin (direct).
