Executive Summary

▾

What Joy Is

Event planning platform (weddings, corporate). 55M users, 150+ countries. YC W16, $108M raised, ~$65M revenue. Their hotel product auto-populates accommodations near event venues.

What They Want

TSK luxury properties listed on their accommodation platform. Also exploring an AI partnership where TSK leverages Joy's event planning AI for guest concierge.

What We Want

Distribution channel to UHNW event planners. Access to Joy's AI and event data for a TSK-branded luxury concierge. Rev-share model on both.

Where It Stands

Richie is sending the distribution agreement for redline. AI deal structure is undefined. Next step is reviewing the agreement and scheduling a follow-up on AI terms.

Key Takeaway

The distribution deal is real and worth pursuing. Destination weddings are a strong channel for luxury villas. The AI partnership needs more diligence. Joy's AI is assembled from off-the-shelf tools (Intercom Fin, OpenAI), not proprietary. The value is in their data, not their tech. Typical customer budget ($35-45K) may be below TSK's floor for top-tier properties.

Deal Structure

▾

Two separate deals. Distribution is near-term. AI is exploratory.

Deal 1: Distribution (Accommodation Listings)

  • Mechanism: TSK properties appear when a Joy user's event is near a TSK location
  • Integration: Richie said via "Zentrum Hub." Research found no Joy-ZentrumHub connection. Need to clarify the actual integration path.
  • Revenue model: Richie asked commission vs. net rate. TSK is pushing rev-share.
  • Status: Agreement coming from Richie for redline

Deal 2: AI Partnership

  • Concept: TSK leverages Joy's AI capabilities for a luxury guest concierge
  • Reality check: Joy's support AI is Intercom Fin (off-the-shelf, $0.99/resolution). Product AI is OpenAI. The value is in Joy's data layer (event history, customer behavior, supplier network), not the AI models.
  • Structure: TBD. See AI partnership models below.
  • Status: Exploratory. No terms discussed yet.

Potential Risks

▾
Budget mismatch. Joy's typical customer spends $35-45K on the entire event. TSK's ultra-luxury properties start well above that for accommodation alone. This channel may only work for mid-tier TSK offerings, not flagship properties.
AI is not proprietary. Joy uses off-the-shelf tools (Intercom Fin, OpenAI). TSK could license the same tools directly. An "AI partnership" needs to deliver access to Joy's data and workflows, not just the AI interface.
Multiple data claims unverified. 55M users, 150+ countries, 400K daily pageviews, Airbnb investment, VRBO partnership. None confirmed by independent research. Need validation before presenting to investors or partners.
Integration path unclear. Richie said "Zentrum Hub" but no Joy-ZentrumHub relationship exists. Joy uses Logicbroker for e-commerce only. Hotel integration layer is unknown. Could affect timeline and cost.
Brand positioning. ZentrumHub (if used) is an OTA aggregator. Listing TSK on mass-market OTA channels could dilute the ultra-luxury brand.
AI deal complexity. Licensing, white-label, rev-share, co-development all have different IP, data, and cost implications. No clarity yet on what Joy would actually deliver.

▾

  • Built by Intercom. Commercially available. Not exclusive to Joy.

Reference: AI Partnership Models

Options for structuring the AI component of the Joy partnership. Based on industry benchmarks and IDC 2026 projections.

ModelHow It WorksCostTSK Fit
Licensing TSK licenses Joy's AI + data layer. TSK owns the guest interface. Fixed annual fee + per-query overage Good control. Need clear data rights.
