Platform Overview
What DealMaker Is

DealMaker is North America's largest retail capital-raising platform. It provides white-labeled, end-to-end technology infrastructure for companies raising capital from both accredited and non-accredited investors. The platform handles investor acquisition, securities management, payment processing, KYC/AML compliance, and ongoing investor relations.

Key point: DealMaker provides the technology and compliance rails. The company (TSK) owns the strategy, content, investor relationships, and raise outcome. DealMaker's terms explicitly state that companies bear primary responsibility for the success of their raise. Why DealMaker

COO Deliverable Ownership
Proposed framework. The following is based on DealMaker's published process and typical raise operations. It is NOT an assertion of what K has assigned. Validate and refine this in Monday's meeting.
COO Role in a Capital Raise

In a typical DealMaker engagement, the COO is the operational backbone of the raise. You don't own the investor pitch (that's CEO/CRO) or the financials (that's CFO). You own the delivery machine — ensuring every deliverable hits its deadline, every team member knows what they owe, and nothing falls through the cracks.

  • You own: Timeline management, deliverable tracking, internal coordination, operational readiness, process documentation
  • You monitor: Investor materials (CRO drafts), financial statements (CFO prepares), legal filings (GC/counsel manages), portal build (DealMaker executes)
  • You escalate: Missed deadlines, scope changes, blockers, resource gaps, any use of Steve's name/bio without signed agreement

What Worked for EnergyX
  • • Webinars were massive: 10 sessions, 9,972 registrants, $4.3M+ generated from webinar audiences alone
  • • Community-first approach: Converted 7,000 existing supporters into investors — most impactful channel
  • • Hybrid funding: Retail (DealMaker) + institutional (GM, Posco Holdings, DOE) in parallel
  • • Founder directly engaged: CEO Teague Egan partnered closely with DealMaker team
  • • Mobile-optimized: Investors completed checkout from phones in under a minute
Lessons for TSK
  • • Existing community (waitlist, past inquiries, advisors) is the strongest capital source
  • • Live events (webinars) significantly outperform passive marketing for lifestyle/luxury brands
  • • Mobile checkout optimization is non-negotiable
  • • Institutional + retail hybrid accelerates both timeline and credibility
  • • Founder/CEO visibility matters — K needs to be front-and-center for investor engagement
  • • Open question for TSK: Does TSK have a community to convert? Any waitlist, mailing list, or warm leads?
